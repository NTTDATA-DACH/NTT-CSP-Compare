SOV-01 Legal Seat and Ownership Structure
View Control Description
Criterion: The Cloud Service Provider (CSP) must have its head office and effective management within the European Union (EU) or the EEA. Majority (>50%) of share capital and voting rights must be held by natural or legal persons based in the EU/EEA. It must be legally ensured that no effective control by entities from third countries is possible. Additional Note: Joint ventures are permitted as long as the European partner has operational control and veto rights for security-relevant decisions.
-10
Reasoning

Microsoft Azure is developed and operated by Microsoft Corporation, which is headquartered in Redmond, Washington, USA. Therefore, Azure fundamentally fails the core requirement of this control.

  • Head Office & Ownership: Microsoft is a US-based, publicly traded corporate entity, meaning its majority voting rights and effective management reside outside the EU/EEA.
  • Third-Country Control: Because the parent company is governed by US law, it is legally subject to third-country control.
  • Impact of Sovereign Offerings: Even when utilizing the Microsoft Cloud for Sovereignty, the legal entity maintaining operational control of the hyperscale infrastructure remains the US-based Microsoft Corporation. As such, the baseline state is in direct opposition to the control.
 -10
Reasoning

Assessment: Standard GCP fails the legal seat and ownership criteria.

  • Ownership & Legal Seat: Google Cloud Platform is operated by Google LLC, a subsidiary of Alphabet Inc. Both are headquartered in the US, giving them effective control.
  • Sovereign Offerings: To mitigate this, Google partners with European firms (e.g., T-Systems in Germany, Thales in France) for 'Sovereign Cloud powered by Google Cloud' solutions where the European partner holds operational control. However, the baseline GCP offering evaluated here remains under US ownership.
SOV-02 Data Location and Jurisdiction
View Control Description
Criterion: All customer data, metadata, authentication data, and backups must be stored and processed exclusively on servers within the EU/EEA. The contractually agreed jurisdiction and applicable law must exclusively be that of an EU/EEA member state. Additional Note: Data transfer to third countries must be technically prevented. Exceptions require explicit initiation by the customer.
-5
Reasoning

Azure offers robust data residency commitments through its EU Data Boundary (Phase 3 completed in February 2025), but it falls short of the strict sovereignty requirements for exclusive EU jurisdiction and absolute technical prevention.

  • Data Location: The EU Data Boundary ensures that customer data, pseudonymized personal data, and professional services data (from technical support) are stored and processed within the EU and EFTA regions.
  • Jurisdiction & Legal Control: Despite local storage, Microsoft remains a US entity. Consequently, the jurisdiction is not exclusively tied to an EU/EEA member state, creating conflicting legal obligations under US laws like the CLOUD Act.
  • Technical Prevention: Standard Azure relies heavily on policy controls (such as Sovereign Landing Zone policies) rather than physical hard barriers preventing all data transfer by the operator.

Therefore, Azure receives a -5 (Significant Non-Compliance), as the localized data boundary exists but fails strict exclusive jurisdiction criteria.

View Control Description
Criterion: The use of software or hardware with integrated mechanisms for remote deactivation, functional restriction, or license blocking (killswitch) by the manufacturer is inadmissible. The operation of the cloud platform must be fully possible even if the connection to the license servers or management interfaces of the technology provider is permanently interrupted. Additional Note: Independence from external license servers ('Phone Home' compulsion) must be technically proven. Contractual assurances alone are not enough.
-10
Reasoning

Azure operates as a deeply integrated ecosystem that relies entirely on a central control plane, making it fundamentally vulnerable to remote disconnection.

  • Centralized Control Plane: Standard Azure infrastructure is completely dependent on Microsoft's global management plane, including Azure Resource Manager (ARM) and Microsoft Entra ID for authentication, licensing, and resource provisioning.
  • Remote Shutdown Risks: If connection to these global management interfaces is permanently interrupted, the cloud platform cannot continue to operate independently. Azure maintains a strict 'phone home' requirement for core operations.

Because technical independence from the external provider's global control plane is impossible for standard Azure, it receives a score of -10 (Absolute Non-Compliance).

 -10
Reasoning

  • Killswitch & Phone Home: GCP is a multi-tenant public cloud requiring continuous connectivity to Google's management and billing servers. It cannot operate independently if the connection is permanently interrupted.
