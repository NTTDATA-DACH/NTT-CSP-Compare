Overarching Summary

The comparison presents a distinct strategic dichotomy between agility and sovereignty. Scaleway (Service A) operates as a mature 'Cloud Challenger,' offering a full-stack, innovation-driven platform. It wins decisively on cost-efficiency (often 50-70% cheaper), developer experience, and modern features like Serverless, AI, and managed observability. However, its value proposition is compromised by recent concerns regarding data durability and platform stability. Conversely, STACKIT (Service B) positions itself as a 'Sovereign Enterprise Vault.' Built on open standards (OpenStack, Gardener, Vault), it offers superior portability and compliance (backed by the Schwarz Group), making it ideal for regulated German workloads. However, it suffers from significant maturity gaps (Alpha/Beta services), high friction, and an expensive enterprise pricing model.

Strategic Recommendation: Adopt Scaleway for the majority of greenfield applications, development environments, and cost-sensitive scale-outs, provided architecture includes application-level redundancy to mitigate provider reliability risks. Reserve STACKIT exclusively for workloads where strict German data sovereignty or OpenStack interoperability are non-negotiable requirements.

Domain-Specific Summaries

Compute

Scaleway is the superior choice for modern, agile infrastructure, offering advanced features like Serverless, AI supercomputing, and ARM architectures at aggressively low prices. It prioritizes innovation but locks users into a proprietary API. STACKIT is a conservative Infrastructure-as-a-Service provider; while it lacks feature depth and hardware diversity, it excels in portability due to its reliance on standard OpenStack APIs. STACKIT is suitable for standard enterprise VMs requiring compliance, while Scaleway is the definitive choice for performance-per-euro and technical breadth.

Storage

This domain highlights a critical trade-off between cost and safety. Scaleway offers a comprehensive, S3-compatible suite (including Glacier) at market-leading prices, but suffers from severe trust issues due to recent data durability incidents. STACKIT offers a technically superior and more reliable backend designed for enterprise compliance and SAP archival, but at a significant price premium and with higher operational friction. For mission-critical data retention, STACKIT is the safer bet; for ephemeral or replicable data, Scaleway offers unbeatable value.

Networking

Scaleway delivers a mature, cloud-native networking experience with modern features like IPv6, managed gateways, and a simplified billing model that eliminates common bandwidth costs. It is user-friendly but proprietary. STACKIT adheres to standard OpenStack networking, offering high architectural portability but lagging in feature polish (e.g., IPv6 maturity) and retaining a traditional, potentially expensive ingress/egress billing model. Scaleway is operationally superior, while STACKIT favors hybrid-cloud interoperability.

Container Operations

Scaleway Kapsule is the accessible, cost-effective choice, offering a free control plane and a polished user experience, though it has struggled with stability (cascading failures). STACKIT manages Kubernetes via the robust Gardener project, offering distinct advantages in fleet management and portability (such as hibernation). However, STACKIT's steep management fees and the current instability of its Beta registry service make it a difficult sell for general use unless the Gardener architecture is a specific strategic requirement.

Databases and Big Data

Scaleway leads in innovation and developer experience, offering Serverless SQL, pgvector support, and cost-effective managed instances suitable for modern applications. STACKIT takes a rigid, enterprise-first approach, enforcing expensive High Availability clusters by default. While STACKIT excels in specific niches—such as Dremio for Data Lakehouses and a standards-compliant RabbitMQ service—Scaleway remains the more versatile and affordable platform for general-purpose database and analytics workloads.

AI Services

Scaleway provides a comprehensive AI ecosystem, effectively bridging the gap between consuming APIs and managing custom model inference. It offers an excellent onboarding experience with free tiers and serverless flexibility. STACKIT focuses on a 'Model-as-a-Service' approach; while it lacks the tooling for easy custom model deployment, it is surprisingly cost-effective for high-volume inference on massive open-source models (e.g., Llama 70B), making it a viable specialist option for heavy inference workloads.

Security and Governance

Scaleway offers a mature, production-ready suite with stable IAM, Audit Trails, and highly affordable Secret/Key management. It is integrated and ready for immediate use. STACKIT is currently in a transitional phase; while its use of the standard HashiCorp Vault API offers excellent portability, its core IAM is experimental, and its governance tools are immature (Alpha status). Scaleway is the reliable choice for immediate security needs, whereas STACKIT is positioned for future open-standard compliance.

Monitoring

Scaleway Cockpit is a modern, serverless observability solution based on Grafana Mimir, offering zero-configuration ingestion and a pay-per-use model that is free for platform metrics. STACKIT relies on a legacy Managed Service Provider model, requiring users to provision and pay for dedicated observability instances. Scaleway is vastly superior for agility and cost, while STACKIT is only relevant for legacy architectures requiring dedicated resource isolation.

Edge and IoT

Scaleway provides a simple, highly affordable edge caching layer integrated with its storage and compute, ideal for accelerating internal resources. STACKIT offers a more robust, standalone CDN architecture capable of serving external origins with strict sovereignty features (geofencing). Choose Scaleway for simple app acceleration and cost control; choose STACKIT if the workload requires a true, origin-agnostic CDN with guaranteed European data residency.

Technical Analysis

Scaleway (Service A) is significantly ahead in terms of feature breadth and innovation. It has successfully transitioned from a bare-metal provider to a full-stack cloud with Serverless, Managed AI, and diverse compute architectures (ARM, RISC-V). The contrast is stark: STACKIT embraces the industry standard, while Scaleway builds a walled garden.

While its billing is transparent and follows a standard hourly model, it lacks the aggressive low-end tiers and the cost-performance ratio of Scaleway's ARM or Development instances. STACKIT is a choice for compliance-heavy workloads, whereas Scaleway is the optimal choice for cost-conscious startups and general-purpose infrastructure.

Service B (STACKIT) adopts a more bifurcated architecture, splitting 'Standard Object Storage' and 'Compliant Archiving' into separate products. While this creates a Developer Experience (DX) friction (you cannot simply set a lifecycle rule to 'Glacier' in the same bucket as easily), it isolates the complexity and risk. STACKIT's backend appears more stable, utilizing a robust (likely Ceph-based) architecture that prioritizes consistency over feature velocity. Consequently, STACKIT receives a positive score for providing a dependable, albeit basic, storage utility, whereas Scaleway's 'advanced' features currently carry unacceptable operational risk.

Service B (STACKIT) has higher lock-in friction. Both services utilize the S3 API standard, ensuring basic data portability. However, Service A (Scaleway) adheres strictly to the AWS S3 model for storage classes, allowing users to use standard tools (like aws s3api or Terraform) to manage lifecycle policies that transition data to Cold storage (Glacier). Service B (STACKIT) separates its 'Archiving Service' from its standard 'Object Storage'. This means a user relying on STACKIT for long-term retention implements a proprietary or non-standard workflow to move data between these services, increasing the engineering effort required to migrate away to a standard S3 provider later.

Scaleway also provides a recurring 75 GB free egress allowance, which covers many small-to-medium use cases entirely.

STACKIT Object Storage follows a more traditional, enterprise-tier pricing model comparable to AWS S3 Standard. With storage pricing estimated around €0.027/GB/mo (based on hourly rates), it is roughly 2x more expensive than Scaleway's Multi-AZ tier and nearly 4x more expensive than Scaleway's One Zone tier. STACKIT focuses on data sovereignty and enterprise compliance (Schwarz Group) rather than competing on raw price. For a typical startup or value-focused workload, STACKIT offers significantly lower value for money compared to Scaleway.

Technical Analysis

Comparison Summary: The comparison reveals a fundamental divergence in design philosophy. Scaleway Glacier is a true Infrastructure-as-a-Service (IaaS) primitive: a flexible, programmable storage class designed for builders. STACKIT Archiving is a Compliance-as-a-Service product: a rigid vault designed for auditors and SAP administrators. For the vast majority of cloud architects and developers, Scaleway is technically superior due to its interoperability.

  • Standards & Versatility: Scaleway uses the industry-standard S3 protocol. A developer can switch a flag in Terraform (e.g., storage_class = "GLACIER") and the infrastructure adapts. STACKIT Archiving requires learning a proprietary REST API or implementing SAP-specific protocols, making it unusable for standard cloud-native backup workflows (e.g., storing Prometheus metrics or Postgres logs) without significant custom development.
  • Developer Experience (DX): Scaleway integrates into the modern stack. Users can browse archives via CLI, set lifecycle policies, and retrieve data using standard libraries. STACKIT's service is a 'black box' designed to receive data streams from ERP systems and lock them away. The lack of standard tooling (rclone, s3cmd) support for the Archiving service is a major technical deficit for general utility.
  • Performance & Friction: Scaleway offers a predictable, tiered retrieval model. STACKIT's focus on 'audit-proof' storage imposes friction by design (WORM), which, while valuable for legal compliance, is a hindrance for general technical operations (e.g., rotating backup snapshots).

Score Justification: The score of -6 reflects that STACKIT Archiving is Noticeably Inferior for general cloud architecture use cases due to the lack of standard S3 compatibility and high barrier to entry (proprietary API), despite its niche strength in SAP environments.

Lock-in Analysis

Scaleway (Service A): +10 (Zero Lock-in). Scaleway utilizes the industry-standard S3 API. Data can be migrated out using any commodity tool (rclone, boto3) simply by issuing retrieval requests. The exit cost is purely bandwidth and retrieval fees, with zero technical refactoring required.

STACKIT (Service B): -8 (High Proprietary Lock-in). The STACKIT Archiving Service uses a distinct, non-S3 REST API and proprietary SAP interfaces. Migrating data out of STACKIT Archiving would require writing custom scripts to interact with their specific API to retrieve objects, stripping away the proprietary metadata, and re-uploading to a new target. Furthermore, the WORM nature of the service may technically prevent deletion or modification until retention periods expire, creating a 'forced' lock-in based on legal configuration.

Pricing Analysis

Scaleway Glacier is the clear winner for cost-efficient archiving, offering a true "cold storage" price point of approximately €0.002 per GB/month. This is roughly 10x cheaper than STACKIT's offering for equivalent storage volume. Scaleway also includes a generous 75 GB free egress allowance, making it ideal for startups with typical backup or log retention needs.

  • Scaleway: Uses a classic deep archive model. You pay a rock-bottom price for storage but incur a €0.009/GB fee to restore data, with a retrieval delay of 24-48 hours. This model is perfect for data you rarely touch (Write Once, Read Never).
  • STACKIT: Their "Archiving" service is positioned more as a compliance and enterprise feature (WORM, SAP integration) rather than a low-cost bit bucket. The pricing appears to align closer to standard object storage rates (~€0.027/GB/month), which is significantly more expensive for pure data hoarding capability.

For a typical startup looking to store backups or logs cheaply, Scaleway provides massive value. STACKIT is only cost-competitive if the specific regulatory compliance features (like audit-proof SAP archiving) reduce other administrative overheads.

Technical Analysis

Performance & Architecture: STACKIT demonstrates a clear technical superiority in 'hard specs.' While Scaleway caps out at 15,000 IOPS (Block 15K tier), STACKIT's 'Premium-Performance' classes scale up to 40,000 IOPS, making it far more suitable for demanding IO-intensive databases. Furthermore, STACKIT offers Multi-AZ synchronous mirroring (Metro Volumes), a critical enterprise feature for zero-RPO disaster recovery that Scaleway lacks (Scaleway Block Storage is strictly Zonal with 3 intra-zone replicas). Scaleway's advice for Multi-AZ resilience is 'application-level replication,' which shifts the burden to the user.

Reliability & SLA: Scaleway's published SLA for Block Storage is 99.9%, which is surprisingly low for a core cloud primitive (industry standard is typically 99.99%). Combined with recent community reports of instability in their storage layer (2024 incidents), this lowers their technical reliability score. STACKIT's architecture, leveraging multi-AZ mirroring, inherently supports higher availability tiers.

Summary: STACKIT is not just a 'Sovereign' alternative; it is technically more performant and resilient than Scaleway's offering, justifying a score of +7.

Lock-in Analysis

API Standards: STACKIT is built directly on OpenStack Cinder. This means users can utilize standard OpenStack clients, libraries, and Terraform providers with minimal modification, facilitating easy migration to/from other OpenStack-based private or public clouds. Scaleway, while offering standard CSI drivers for Kubernetes, relies on a proprietary API for infrastructure management. Migrating away from Scaleway requires rewriting infrastructure-as-code definitions, whereas STACKIT adheres to a widely adopted open standard.

Pricing Analysis

Scaleway is the overwhelming winner for typical startup workloads due to its aggressive performance-per-euro ratio. Its standard Block Storage offering provides 5,000 IOPS and NVMe backing for approximately €0.086/GB/month. This 'batteries-included' approach means a 100GB database volume costs roughly €8.60/month with production-ready speeds.

STACKIT utilizes a bifurcated model that separates Capacity from Performance. While its 'Premium-Capacity' tier is nominally cheaper at ~€0.066/GB/month, it lacks the high IOPS guarantees. To match Scaleway's 5,000 IOPS, a user must likely provision a specific 'Premium-Performance' class (e.g., Performance 6), which incurs a flat hourly fee (e.g., ~€0.10/hour or ~€73/month) on top of capacity costs. This makes STACKIT significantly more expensive for small-to-medium datasets requiring low latency.

For a startup, Scaleway offers enterprise-grade speed at commodity prices, while STACKIT's model is geared towards large-scale enterprise deployments where paying flat fees for dedicated performance lanes is acceptable.

This allows users to switch CDN providers (e.g., to Cloudflare or Akamai) without necessarily migrating their backend infrastructure, significantly reducing exit costs.

Pricing Analysis

Scaleway Edge Services offers a highly aggressive pricing model tailored for cost-efficiency. Its subscription-based approach starts at just €0.99/month for 100GB of cache egress, which effectively works out to ~€0.01/GB. Even the overage rate is very low at €0.0135/GB. This makes it one of the most affordable options in the market, competing with budget CDNs like BunnyCDN while offering integrated cloud features.

STACKIT CDN, consistent with its enterprise positioning, utilizes a standard Pay-As-You-Go model. While specific per-GB rates are dynamically calculated, enterprise European cloud providers typically charge in the range of €0.05 - €0.09 per GB (inferred from standard regional pricing structures and snippet data suggesting billing resolution per MB). Without a bundled 'starter' allowance, a startup with moderate traffic (e.g., 500GB) would likely pay significantly more on STACKIT than the flat €12.99 Professional plan on Scaleway (which covers 1TB).

For a typical startup workload, Scaleway is the clear winner on value, offering near-unbeatable bandwidth rates and a predictable billing structure.
