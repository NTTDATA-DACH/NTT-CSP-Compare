By utilizing the Clever Cloud engine, it offers a "git-push" deployment model that is lightweight and highly praised by the developer community. It supports polyglot applications with minimal friction. Conversely, STACKIT relies on Cloud Foundry (CF), a standard that, while robust, is facing declining market share and "heavier" operational overhead. The recent sunsetting of STACKIT's "Dedicated CF" preview suggests roadmap consolidation, making it a riskier bet for long-term innovation compared to Scaleway's vibrant ecosystem.

2. Cost Efficiency & Economics

Scaleway wins decisively on economics for modern workloads. Its scale-to-zero capability and per-second billing mean idle applications cost nothing—a critical advantage for startups and variable workloads. The generous recurring free tier further sweetens the deal. STACKIT operates on a traditional billing model (per MB-Hour) where costs accrue as long as the service is deployed, regardless of traffic. For sporadic or early-stage applications, Scaleway is significantly cheaper.

3. Strategic Trade-offs: Innovation vs. Sovereignty

The only strong argument for STACKIT is Digital Sovereignty and Portability. As a Schwarz Group entity, STACKIT is ideal for German enterprises requiring strict data residency and adherence to Cloud Foundry open standards (low lock-in). Scaleway, while European, relies on a proprietary orchestration layer (medium lock-in), though the underlying code remains standard.

CTO Verdict

Select Scaleway for all greenfield projects, startups, and agile teams requiring cost efficiency and modern tooling. Relegate STACKIT solely to scenarios where German data sovereignty or legacy Cloud Foundry manifest compatibility is a non-negotiable compliance requirement.
