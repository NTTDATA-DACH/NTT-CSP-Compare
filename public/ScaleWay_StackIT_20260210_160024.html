Overarching Summary

The strategic comparison between Scaleway and STACKIT represents a choice between a mature, commercial 'European Hyperscaler' and a specialized 'Sovereign Compliance' provider. Scaleway is the superior choice for 80-90% of business cases, particularly for digital natives and scale-ups. It dominates in Developer Experience (DX), feature velocity (Serverless, AI, Managed Services), and cost-efficiency, often undercutting STACKIT by 3x-4x. Scaleway functions as a cohesive Cloud Platform. STACKIT, conversely, functions as a robust Virtual Data Center. Its primary value proposition lies in the Schwarz Group ecosystem (Retail/Enterprise sovereignty) and specific high-resilience engineering features like Metro Availability Zones (synchronous replication) and Managed Vault. Recommendation: Standardize on Scaleway for agility, innovation, and cost management. Reserve STACKIT exclusively for workloads requiring strict German physical data residency, zero-RPO synchronous storage replication, or integration with the Schwarz retail ecosystem.

Databases and Big Data

Messaging

AI Services

STACKIT is currently limited to a 'Model-as-a-Service' API catalog, making it viable for generic chatbot integration but insufficient for proprietary AI development.

Monitoring

Scaleway offers a mature, production-ready observability suite that is cost-effective and supports data export. STACKIT's monitoring stack is currently in a risky state (Preview status, no telemetry backups, no data export), making it unsuitable for mission-critical historical analysis.

Edge and IoT

Scaleway provides a mature, integrated, and highly affordable Edge/CDN portfolio. STACKIT's offering is nascent and lacks the operational history and feature depth required for modern content delivery.

Technical Analysis

The Battle of Resilience vs. Performance Transparency.

This comparison reveals a distinct divergence in architectural philosophy. Scaleway (Service A) prioritizes raw performance transparency and developer ergonomics. Their offering is strictly Zonal (standard for most cloud block storage), but they execute it flawlessly with explicit IOPS tiers (5k/15k) and a 99.99% SLA that relies on triple replication within the data center. It is the better choice for high-performance databases (DBs) where latency is king and the application handles HA (e.g., via DB replication).

STACKIT (Service B), however, introduces a massive Hard Spec advantage with its Metro Availability Zone capability. Unlike Scaleway, STACKIT offers synchronous replication across physically separate Availability Zones.
