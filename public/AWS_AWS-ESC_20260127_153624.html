Overarching Summary

The comprehensive audit of AWS services reveals a definitive strategic bifurcation between the Global Commercial Cloud and the European Sovereign Cloud (ESC). Across every domain, the underlying technical engines are largely identical; however, the ESC consistently imposes a 15-30% 'sovereignty premium,' reduces feature availability (particularly in CI/CD and AI), and eliminates global economies of scale like Spot liquidity and volume discounts. Therefore, the strategic directive is to standardize on AWS Commercial for all workloads to maximize ROI and operational agility. We will treat the Sovereign Cloud not as a technical tier, but strictly as a compliance containment zone, utilized only when legal mandates explicitly prohibit the use of global infrastructure. Furthermore, across the portfolio, we will prioritize foundational 'engine' services (e.g., Bedrock Core, EKS, ECS) over high-cost managed abstractions (e.g., Bedrock Data Automation, SageMaker Canvas) to maintain profit margins and architectural control.
